# Content Type Templates

Reference structures for STAGE 3 (Outline). Adapt to the topic and angle — these are starting points, not rigid rules.

---

## Blog Post

**Goal:** inform, entertain, or argue a point. Builds trust and authority.

```
1. Headline
   - Specific, not vague. Promises something concrete.
   - Avoid: "The Ultimate Guide to X", "Everything You Need to Know About Y"
   - Prefer: "Why X Fails (and What to Do Instead)", "I Spent 3 Months Doing X — Here's What I Learned"

2. Lead paragraph (1-3 sentences)
   - Sets up the tension, problem, or angle immediately
   - Never start with "In this article, I will..."
   - Hook: surprising fact, bold claim, question, or short story

3. Section 1 — Context or Problem
   - What's the situation? Why does this matter?
   - Ground it in reality — data, example, anecdote

4. Section 2 — Core Argument / Main Content
   - The meat of the piece
   - Where the personal angle goes if you have one

5. Section 3 — Evidence or Examples
   - Concrete proof of Section 2
   - Cite sources from research (STAGE 2)

6. Section 4 — Counterpoint or Nuance (optional)
   - What's the other side? What does this NOT apply to?
   - Makes the piece feel more honest and less one-sided

7. Closing paragraph
   - Strong final thought — not a summary
   - Leave the reader with something to think about, or a clear next action
   - No "in conclusion"

8. CTA (subtle)
   - One sentence: share, follow, related post link, or question to comment on
```

**Length guidance:** 800–1,500 words for most blog posts. Long-form: 2,000–3,500 for pillar content.

## About Page (Person or Company)

**Goal:** build trust and connection. Make the reader care before they check credentials.

```
1. Opening hook
   - Surprising fact, bold claim, or question about who this is
   - NOT: "Hi, I'm João and I'm a developer with 10 years of experience"
   - YES: "I've rebuilt the same product three times. The third time, it worked."

2. Story
   - Origin: how did this start?
   - Turning point: what changed the trajectory?
   - Why this, why now?

3. Values / Mission (2-3 max)
   - Specific and concrete — not "we value innovation"
   - "We don't ship until we've used it ourselves" beats "we value quality"

4. Social proof / proof of work
   - For a person: notable projects, clients, numbers
   - For a company: logos, metrics, testimonial quote
   - Keep it short — 2-4 items max

5. CTA
   - One clear action: "let's talk", "see our work", contact link
   - Match tone — casual CTA for casual about page, professional for formal
```

**Length guidance:** 250–600 words. About pages are scanned, not read.

## Landing Page / Product Page

**Goal:** convert. Every section earns the next click.

```
1. Hero
   - Headline: what this is + who it's for (one sentence max)
   - Subheadline: the one thing that makes this different
   - Primary CTA button: action verb + outcome ("Start for free", "Get the report")

2. Problem
   - The world without this product/service
   - Be specific about the pain — no generic "struggle"
   - 2-4 sentences or 3 bullet points

3. Solution — How it works
   - 3 steps or 3 features max (more = cognitive overload)
   - Each point: what it does + why it matters
   - Visual placeholder note if relevant: [screenshot/icon goes here]

4. Social proof
   - 2-3 testimonials with name, role, company (no anonymous quotes)
   - Or: key metrics ("10,000 users in 6 months", "4.8★ on G2")
   - Or: logos of recognizable clients/partners

5. Objection handling
   - 2-3 "but what about..." addressed directly
   - Format: objection as question → direct answer
   - Common: price, time, trust, complexity

6. Final CTA
   - Repeat the primary CTA from Hero
   - Add a framing line above: create urgency or reduce risk ("No credit card required", "Cancel anytime")

7. Optional FAQ (3-5 questions)
   - Address the real blockers to buying/signing up
   - Keep answers under 3 sentences each
```

**Length guidance:** No fixed length — every word must earn its place. Cut anything that doesn't move the reader toward the CTA.

## FAQ Section

**Goal:** pre-empt objections, reduce support load, improve SEO.

```
Structure:
- 5-8 questions
- Order: most common → most nuanced
- Answers: 2-4 sentences, conversational register
- Never open with "Great question!" or any filler
- Never answer with a question (e.g., "Why would you want to know?")

Format:
**Question text here?**
Answer in 2-4 sentences. Direct, specific. No preamble.

SEO note:
- Question phrasing should match how people actually search
- Use exact question format ("How do I...", "What is...", "Can I...")
- Each answer can include 1 internal or external link
```

**Length guidance:** Each answer: 40–100 words. Total FAQ section: 400–800 words.

## Social Media Posts

### LinkedIn

**Goal:** professional credibility + engagement. Readers scan, then read if hooked.

```
Structure:
- Line 1: the hook — bold claim, surprising stat, or question (this is what shows before "see more")
- [blank line]
- Paragraph 1: context or story (3-5 lines)
- [blank line]  
- Paragraph 2: insight or lesson (3-4 lines)
- [blank line]
- Closing line: call to action, question, or memorable thought
- [blank line]
- Hashtags: 1-3 only, relevant (not #marketing #business #growth)
```

**Length:** 150-300 words optimal. Over 600 = drops off.
**Tone:** follow selection from INTAKE, but LinkedIn always leans slightly more professional than other channels.

---

### Twitter / X

**Goal:** shareability. Punchy, one idea per tweet.

```
Single tweet:
- Under 270 chars (leave room for retweets)
- One idea, stated directly
- No throat-clearing ("I think that...", "It's worth saying that...")

Thread (for longer content):
- Tweet 1: the hook (most important — this is what people RT)
- Tweets 2–N: each tweet must work standalone (someone might see it out of context)
- Number format: 1/ 2/ 3/ ... (not "Thread 🧵")
- Final tweet: summary + CTA or question
- Max 8-10 tweets before it becomes a blog post (write a blog post instead)
```

---

### Instagram

**Goal:** visual-first, caption supports the image.

```
Caption structure:
- Line 1: hook (shows before "more" — make it count)
- 3-4 sentences: story, context, or value
- CTA: question, "save this", "tag someone", or link-in-bio
- [blank line]
- Hashtags: 3-5, specific and relevant (not #instagood #love)
  - Mix: 1 broad (#webdesign), 1 niche (#freelancedesignerportugal), 1 brand or topic-specific
```

**Length:** 100-200 words max. Instagram captions compete with images — keep it tight.
